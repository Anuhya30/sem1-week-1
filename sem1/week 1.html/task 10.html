H1 heading is used for Main Title

The H1 tag is reserved for the main title of your page or document. This should be a concise and descriptive phrase that clearly indicates the primary topic. Content here sets the tone for the entire page and should be both informative and engaging, often incorporating important keywords for SEO.

H2 is used for Section Headings

H2 tags are used for major section headings within your content. These headings should break up your content into logical sections, helping to organize the material for readability. Use H2s for key topics or themes that support the H1 title, and make them descriptive enough to give readers a clear idea of what to expect in that section.

H3 tag is used for Subsections

H3 tags serve as subheadings under H2 sections. They help further divide content into more detailed subsections. Use H3s to introduce specific points or aspects related to the H2 heading, allowing readers to navigate your content easily. This helps to maintain a clear hierarchy and makes your content more skimmable.

H4 is used for Detailed Points

H4 tags are ideal for breaking down complex information introduced in H3 sections. This level of heading can be used for lists, examples, or detailed explanations that further elaborate on the topics covered in H3. They help to provide depth and clarity, making it easier for readers to digest intricate details.>

H5 tag is used for Minor Sections

H5 tags are used for even more granular subdivisions of content. This level is suitable for smaller, yet still relevant points that support H4 headings. Use H5 for specific case studies, anecdotal evidence, or additional commentary that may not require the same prominence as higher-level headings.

H6 is used for Footnotes or Fine Details

The H6 tag is the least prominent heading level and is used for the smallest sections of content.
